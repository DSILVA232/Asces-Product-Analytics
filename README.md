# Executive Summary 

## Overview Findings
Across 2022 and 2023, Asces Sound demonstrated overall growth in revenue, profitability, and sales volume, with clear regional and seasonal performance patterns.

## Top Revenue Markets: 
Canada generated the highest total revenue across both years, followed closely by the United States and France.

## Seasonality Trends: 
Sales consistently peaked in June and October in both years, indicating stable seasonal demand patterns.

## Year-over-Year Growth:

Profit increased by 7% YoY

Units sold increased by 8% YoY
This suggests growth is primarily volume-driven.

## Customer Segment Performance:

Government remained the largest revenue contributor (above $600K both years).

All other segments remained clustered between $200K–$250K.

All segments increased revenue year over year except Government, which declined slightly.

## Discount Strategy Impact:

High and Medium discount categories account for nearly 70% of total revenue, indicating strong reliance on discounted sales.

Full-price sales contribute only 6.61% of revenue.

Overall, the company is growing steadily, but performance is slightly dependent on key regions and discounted pricing strategies.

# Recomendations

## Prioritize Top Revenue Markets
Increase strategic focus on Canada, the United States, and France, as they are the primary revenue drivers.

## Leverage Seasonal Peaks
Align marketing campaigns, inventory planning, and product launches with recurring June and October sales spikes.

## Reduce Discount Dependency
With nearly 70% of revenue coming from high and medium discounts, evaluate pricing strategy to protect long-term margins.

## Reassess Government Segment Strategy
Investigate the year-over-year decline in Government revenue and reduce overreliance on this segment.

## Maintain Margin Discipline While Scaling Volume
Since growth is primarily volume-driven (8% unit growth vs 7% profit growth), closely monitor profit margins to ensure sustainable expansion.
